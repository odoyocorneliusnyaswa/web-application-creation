Our Academic Philosophy

At Our Lady of Perpetual Help, Ahero Girls' High School, we believe that education is not merely the acquisition of knowledge but the development of the whole person. Our academic program is designed to challenge students intellectually, foster critical thinking skills, and nurture a lifelong love of learning.

We are committed to providing a balanced education that prepares our students for success in higher education and beyond. Our curriculum emphasizes both depth and breadth of knowledge, encouraging students to explore diverse subjects while developing expertise in their areas of interest.

We recognize that each student has unique talents, interests, and learning styles. Our teachers employ diverse teaching methodologies to engage all learners and help them reach their full potential. We maintain small class sizes to ensure personalized attention and create an environment where students feel comfortable asking questions and expressing their ideas.

Technology is integrated throughout our curriculum to enhance learning experiences and prepare students for the digital age. However, we also emphasize the importance of human connection, collaboration, and face-to-face communication in the learning process.

Above all, we strive to instill in our students a sense of intellectual curiosity, academic integrity, and a commitment to using their knowledge and skills to make a positive difference in the world.

Our Curriculum

Our Lady of Perpetual Help follows the 8-4-4 curriculum as prescribed by the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD), offering a comprehensive range of subjects that prepare students for the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examinations.

In Form 1 and Form 2, all students take a broad range of subjects to build a strong foundation across different disciplines:

Languages

  • English
  • Kiswahili

Sciences

  • Mathematics
  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics

Humanities

  • History & Government
  • Geography
  • Religious Education (CRE/IRE)

Technical & Applied

  • Computer Studies
  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • Home Science

In Form 3 and Form 4, students specialize by selecting subjects based on their interests, strengths, and career aspirations. The curriculum is organized as follows:

Compulsory Subjects

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Mathematics

Science Options

Students select at least two:

  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics

Humanities Options

Students select at least one:

  • History & Government
  • Geography
  • Religious Education (CRE/IRE)

Technical & Applied Options

Students may select one:

  • Computer Studies
  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • Home Science

Note: Students take a minimum of 8 subjects for KCSE examinations. Subject selection is done with guidance from teachers and career counselors to ensure alignment with students' career goals.

Academic Support Programs

Remedial Classes

We offer targeted remedial sessions for students who need additional support in specific subjects. These small-group sessions provide personalized attention and help students build confidence in challenging areas.

Peer Tutoring

Our peer tutoring program pairs academically strong students with those seeking help. This creates a collaborative learning environment and benefits both the tutor and the tutee in deepening their understanding.

Study Groups

Structured study groups meet regularly under teacher supervision to review material, prepare for exams, and work on assignments together. These groups foster teamwork and provide mutual support.

Academic Counseling

Our academic counselors work with students to develop effective study habits, time management skills, and learning strategies. They also help students set and achieve academic goals.

Academic Enrichment

Beyond the standard curriculum, we offer various enrichment opportunities to challenge our students and expand their horizons:

Academic Competitions

Students participate in national and regional competitions in science, mathematics, debate, essay writing, and more. These competitions foster healthy competition and allow students to showcase their talents.

Science and Innovation Club

This club engages students in advanced scientific research, experiments, and innovation projects. Members participate in the annual Science Congress and other STEM-related events.

Literary Society

The Literary Society organizes book clubs, creative writing workshops, poetry slams, and literary discussions to nurture a love for literature and enhance language skills.

Model United Nations

Students develop research, public speaking, and diplomatic skills by representing different countries in simulated UN conferences, addressing global issues and proposing solutions.

Entrepreneurship Program

This program teaches business skills through workshops, mentorship, and student-led enterprises. Students learn to develop business plans, manage finances, and market products or services.

Examination and Assessment

At Our Lady of Perpetual Help, we employ a comprehensive assessment system to monitor student progress and provide timely feedback:

Internal Assessments

  • Continuous Assessment Tests (CATs): Regular tests conducted throughout the term to assess understanding of specific topics and concepts.
  • Mid-Term Examinations: Comprehensive assessments conducted in the middle of each term to evaluate progress.
  • End-Term Examinations: Summative assessments at the end of each term covering all material taught during the term.
  • Project-Based Assessments: Assignments that require research, critical thinking, and application of knowledge to real-world problems.

External Examinations

  • Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE): National examinations taken by Form 4 students that determine university placement and career opportunities.
  • Joint Mock Examinations: Collaborative examinations with other schools to prepare students for KCSE and benchmark our performance.

Academic Reports

Detailed academic reports are issued at the end of each term, providing:

  • Subject-by-subject performance analysis
  • Teacher comments and recommendations
  • Comparative performance data
  • Attendance records

Parent-Teacher Consultations

Regular meetings are scheduled to discuss student progress, address concerns, and collaborate on strategies for improvement. Parents can also request additional consultations as needed.

Academic Resources

Library

Our well-stocked library houses over 10,000 books, journals, and reference materials. It provides a quiet study space and access to digital resources. The library is open during school hours and for extended hours during examination periods.

Computer Laboratory

The computer lab is equipped with 50 modern computers with internet access and educational software. Students use this facility for computer studies, research, and digital literacy development.

Science Laboratories

We have separate laboratories for Biology, Chemistry, and Physics, each fully equipped with modern apparatus and materials for practical experiments and demonstrations.

Resource Center

The resource center provides additional learning materials, past papers, model answers, and multimedia resources to supplement classroom learning and exam preparation.

University and Career Guidance

Our comprehensive guidance program helps students make informed decisions about their future education and career paths:

Career Counseling

Our trained counselors provide individual and group sessions to help students explore their interests, strengths, and potential career paths.

University Application Support

We guide students through the university application process, including course selection, application requirements, and scholarship opportunities.

Career Fairs

Annual career fairs bring representatives from universities, colleges, and various professions to interact with our students and provide information about educational and career opportunities.

Alumni Mentorship

We connect current students with alumni working in various fields to provide mentorship, guidance, and real-world insights into different careers.

Industry Visits

Organized visits to companies, hospitals, research institutions, and other workplaces give students firsthand exposure to different professional environments.

Resource Library

Our career resource library contains university prospectuses, career guides, and information about scholarships and financial aid opportunities.
