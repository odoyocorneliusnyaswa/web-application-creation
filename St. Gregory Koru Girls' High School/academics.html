Our Academic Philosophy

Empowering young women through education

At St. Gregory Koru Girls High School, we believe that education is not merely the acquisition of knowledge but the development of critical thinking, creativity, and character. Our academic program is designed to challenge students intellectually while nurturing their unique talents and abilities.

We are committed to providing a holistic education that prepares our students not only for university and career success but also for meaningful lives as ethical leaders and global citizens. Our curriculum balances rigorous academic standards with opportunities for personal growth and exploration.

Our teaching methodology emphasizes active learning, inquiry-based approaches, and the integration of technology. We encourage our students to question, analyze, and create, fostering a love of learning that extends beyond the classroom.

As a Catholic institution, we integrate faith-based values into our academic program, promoting integrity, compassion, and service to others. We believe that true education addresses the whole personâ€”mind, body, and spirit.

Our Curriculum

Comprehensive and balanced education

Our curriculum follows the 8-4-4 system as prescribed by the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD), with additional enrichment programs that enhance our students' learning experience and prepare them for future success.

In Form 1 and 2, all students take a broad range of subjects to build a strong foundation across disciplines before specializing in their areas of interest in the senior years.

Languages

  • English Language and Literature
  • Kiswahili Language and Literature
  • French (Optional)

Mathematics & Sciences

  • Mathematics
  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics

Humanities

  • History & Government
  • Geography
  • Religious Education (CRE/IRE)

Technical & Applied Subjects

  • Computer Studies
  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • Home Science

All Form 1 and 2 students take 13 subjects, with some technical subjects offered as options based on student interest and available resources.

In Form 3 and 4, students specialize by selecting subjects that align with their interests, strengths, and future career aspirations. Each student takes a minimum of 8 subjects, including the compulsory core subjects.

Compulsory Subjects

All students must take:

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Mathematics

Sciences (Select at least 2)

  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics

Humanities (Select at least 1)

  • History & Government
  • Geography
  • Religious Education (CRE/IRE)

Technical & Applied (Optional)

  • Computer Studies
  • Business Studies
  • Agriculture
  • Home Science
  • French

Our guidance counselors work closely with students and parents to select subject combinations that align with students' career aspirations and university requirements.

Enrichment programs are offered through clubs, societies, and dedicated periods within the school timetable. Students are encouraged to participate in at least two enrichment activities.

Academic Support

Ensuring every student succeeds

Remedial Programs

We offer targeted remedial sessions for students who need additional support in specific subjects. Our teachers provide personalized attention to address learning gaps and build confidence.

Study Groups

Structured study groups facilitate collaborative learning and peer support. These groups meet regularly under teacher supervision to review concepts, solve problems, and prepare for examinations.

Learning Support Unit

Our Learning Support Unit assists students with specific learning needs, providing individualized education plans, accommodations, and specialized resources to ensure all students can access the curriculum.

Academic Mentorship

Each student is assigned an academic mentor who monitors their progress, provides guidance, and helps them develop effective study habits and learning strategies.

Academic Enrichment

Beyond the classroom

We believe that learning extends beyond the classroom. Our academic enrichment programs provide opportunities for students to deepen their knowledge, apply their skills in real-world contexts, and explore their interests.

Academic Competitions

Students participate in national and international competitions in mathematics, science, debate, essay writing, and more, challenging themselves and representing our school with distinction.

Educational Trips

Field trips to museums, national parks, historical sites, and industries bring learning to life and provide context for classroom concepts. Our annual educational tour exposes students to different regions and cultures.

Science & Innovation Fair

Our annual Science and Innovation Fair showcases student projects and research, fostering creativity, scientific inquiry, and problem-solving skills while addressing real-world challenges.

Guest Speaker Series

Distinguished professionals, academics, and alumni visit our school to share their expertise and experiences, inspiring our students and broadening their perspectives on career possibilities.

Examination & Assessment

Measuring progress and achievement

At St. Gregory Koru Girls High School, we employ a comprehensive assessment system that measures not only academic knowledge but also critical thinking, problem-solving, and application skills. Our approach to assessment is designed to provide meaningful feedback that guides teaching and learning.

Internal Assessments

  • Continuous Assessment Tests (CATs) conducted throughout the term
  • Mid-term examinations
  • End-of-term examinations
  • Project-based assessments and practical evaluations
  • Oral presentations and group work assessments

External Examinations

  • Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) for Form 4 students
  • Joint mock examinations with neighboring schools
  • Subject-specific competitions and olympiads

Academic Tracking

We maintain detailed records of each student's academic progress through our digital management system. Parents receive regular updates through termly report cards, parent-teacher meetings, and our online parent portal.

Our assessment philosophy emphasizes growth and improvement. We celebrate not only high achievement but also significant progress and effort, recognizing that each student has unique strengths and areas for development.

University & Career Guidance

Preparing for future success

We are committed to helping our students make informed decisions about their future education and career paths. Our comprehensive guidance program provides resources, information, and personalized support throughout their secondary school journey.

Career Counseling

Our trained counselors provide individual guidance to help students explore their interests, strengths, and potential career paths through assessments and personalized consultations.

University Application Support

We guide students through the university application process, including course selection, application requirements, personal statements, and scholarship opportunities.

Career Exposure

Our annual Career Day brings professionals from various fields to share insights about their careers. We also organize workplace visits and job shadowing opportunities.

Mentorship Program

Our alumni mentorship program connects current students with graduates who are established in their careers, providing guidance, inspiration, and networking opportunities.

Resource Center

Our Career Resource Center provides information about universities, courses, career options, and scholarship opportunities, both in print and digital formats.

We offer workshops on essential skills for university and career success, including interview techniques, CV writing, time management, and financial literacy.
